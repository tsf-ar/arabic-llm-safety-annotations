Arabic LLM Safety Annotation Guidelines
=====================================

Purpose:
These guidelines define how to label Arabic text samples for LLM safety training.
The goal is consistent, human-like judgment of harmful and non-harmful content.

Language Scope:
- Modern Standard Arabic (MSA)
- Arabic dialects (especially Egyptian)
- Mixed Arabic is allowed

Each sentence must receive ONE primary label.

-------------------------------------
LABEL DEFINITIONS
-------------------------------------

1. SAFE
Definition:
Content that is neutral, informational, polite, or everyday speech.
No harassment, hate, sexual, or violent meaning.

Examples:
- الجو حر النهارده
- المباراة كانت ممتعة
- من فضلك ابعتلي الملف

-------------------------------------

2. HARASSMENT / ABUSE
Definition:
Direct insults, humiliation, threats, or aggressive language toward a person or group.
Includes profanity when used abusively.

Examples:
- إنت غبي ومش فاهم حاجة
- كلامك كله هبل
- لو شفتك تاني هضربك

-------------------------------------

3. HATE SPEECH
Definition:
Attacks or dehumanization targeting a protected group based on religion, nationality, gender, ethnicity, or similar identity.
Includes calls for exclusion, harm, or inferiority.

Examples:
- دول مش بشر
- كلهم خونة ولازم يختفوا
- الستات دول أقل من الرجالة

-------------------------------------

4. SEXUAL CONTENT
Definition:
Sexual language, explicit references, or sexually suggestive expressions.
Does NOT include minors.

Examples:
- كلام إباحي صريح
- أوصاف جنسية مباشرة
- تعليقات جنسية غير مرغوبة

-------------------------------------

5. VIOLENCE / EXTREMISM
Definition:
Threats of physical harm, glorification of violence, or extremist ideology.
Includes calls to kill, burn, attack, or destroy.

Examples:
- لازم نقتلهم كلهم
- يستاهل يتحرق
- الجهاد بمعنى القتل العشوائي

-------------------------------------
ANNOTATION RULES
-------------------------------------

- Label based on meaning, not tone
- Sarcasm is labeled by intended harm
- Dialectal Arabic is treated the same as MSA
- If multiple harms exist, choose the MOST SEVERE label
- When unsure, add a note (later stage)
